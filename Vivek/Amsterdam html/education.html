The new bus 69, Westtangent: bus connection between Sloterdijk - Schiphol

The new bus line 69, the Westtangent, between Amsterdam Sloterdijk and Schiphol is becoming more reliable and better connected to the timetable because it runs better than normal bus lines. In order to ensure that the bus can drive better, some parts of the route are given a free bus lane and / or extra parking lanes. The stops will have more accessible platforms with more space and easy access for prams and disabled people, panels with dynamic travel information and more bicycle parking spaces. The bus will run during rush hour at least 8 times an hour and outside rush hour at least 6 times an hour. In Measures new box 69 (PDF, 6 MB) you will find all changes and activities that have already been carried out or are yet to be done.

> Read More

When

From December 2020, the new bus line 69 will be taken into use and included in the GVB timetable.

We are working in several places on the new bus line 69 from March 2020 to March 2021.

Route bus 69

The new bus 69 goes via Sloterdijk Station, Plein '40 - '45, Slotermeerlaan, President Allendelaan, Meer en Vaart, Pieter Calandlaan, Louis Davidsstraat, Plesmanlaan, Laan van Vlaanderen, Anderlechtlaan and Oude Haagseweg to Schiphol North and Schiphol Plaza and back.

The 8 stops on the route are close to stops of other bus and tram lines.

Initator

    The Westtangent is an initiative of:
  • the municipality of Amsterdam
  • the transport region of Amsterdam
  • Schiphol
  • the municipality of Haarlemmermeer
  • the province of North Holland

The initiative stems from the plan for better public transport for the Amsterdam city region. The construction of the Westtangent is one of the measures in the Mobility Implementation Agenda of the municipality of Amsterdam.
